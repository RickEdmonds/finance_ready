Introduction

Entrepreneurship is the lifeblood of every economy — entrepreneurs solve problems, improve livelihoods, and drive economic growth. While the journey is tough, those who take total responsibility, persist through hardship, and accept delayed gratification are the ones who ultimately succeed. This framework helps entrepreneurs understand what financiers and investors actually look for when deciding whether to fund a business.

Conclusion

The A–G framework — Affordability, Behaviour, Certainty of cash flows, Delivery capacity, Expertise, Formal support, and Governance — is the lens every serious financier applies. Entrepreneurs who understand and address each dimension will dramatically improve their chances of accessing funding. Problems and setbacks are inevitable; what matters is the direction you are building in. There is always only one way for someone who has hit rock bottom — and that is up.
